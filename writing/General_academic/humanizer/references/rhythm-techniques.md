# Rhythm Techniques

Use this file when the text is technically fine but sounds flat, uniform, or
over-smoothed.

## Main rhythm failures

- too many medium-length sentences in a row
- repeated subject-verb openings
- every paragraph built the same way
- overuse of explanatory tails after commas

## Practical fixes

### Vary sentence length

Break uniform cadence. Mix:

- short sentences for emphasis
- medium sentences for core delivery
- longer sentences only where the thought needs space

Rule of thumb:
Do not leave more than three sentences in a row with near-identical length.

### Vary sentence openings

Avoid repeating:

- `The X...`
- `This...`
- `It is...`
- transition-led openings like `Additionally,` or `However,`

Shift some sentences to open with:

- action
- contrast
- a concrete detail
- a time or condition, if meaningful

### Break the "explain and summarize" loop

AI prose often:

1. states a point
2. explains it
3. restates why it matters

Humans often stop earlier. Cut the third move when it adds nothing.

### Use punctuation conservatively

- periods are usually better than semicolons
- em dashes are fine, but not repeatedly
- parentheses can work for an actual aside, not fake personality

## Voice with restraint

For general prose, a clearer human voice often comes from:

- stronger verbs
- less hedging
- a more visible point of view
- one concrete detail that feels observed

It does not require:

- slang
- filler like "honestly" everywhere
- performative messiness

## Read-aloud test

If the prose sounds like it was optimized for smoothness rather than speech:

- shorten one or two sentences
- cut abstract transitions
- move the key point closer to the end of the sentence
