# Sentence and Paragraph Patterns

Use this file when the structure feels machine-made even after vocabulary cleanup.

## Sentence-level patterns

### Participial trailers

Pattern:
`Main clause, -ing phrase`

Examples:

- "The results improved, suggesting a stronger fit."
- "She opened the file, revealing several errors."

Fix:

- split into two sentences
- make the second clause direct
- keep only if the rhythm truly benefits

### Hedge-led openings

Pattern:

- "It is important to note that..."
- "It could be argued that..."
- "It appears to be the case that..."

Fix:
Delete the throat-clearing and state the claim directly.

### False significance framing

Pattern:

- "marks a pivotal moment"
- "reflects a broader shift"
- "underscores its lasting importance"

Fix:
State what changed, who did it, or why it matters in concrete terms.

### Overbalanced contrast

Pattern:

- "While X is true, Y must also be considered."
- "Not only X, but also Y."

Fix:
Use direct contrast only when the balance is genuinely needed.

## Paragraph-level patterns

### Uniform paragraph length

If every paragraph is roughly the same size, vary it.

### Topic-support-conclusion every time

AI paragraphs often end by summarizing what they just said. Remove the closing
sentence if the paragraph already landed.

### Lists of three everywhere

Break repeated triads. Humans use two-item and four-item groupings all the time.

### Transition-first paragraphs

Avoid opening each paragraph with:

- however
- furthermore
- additionally
- in conclusion

Often the transition can be deleted entirely.

## Structural rewrite guidance

When a paragraph is deeply AI-marked, do not patch line by line. Rewrite the
whole paragraph around:

1. the real claim
2. the concrete evidence or example
3. the minimum needed framing
